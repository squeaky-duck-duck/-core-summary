The big arguments — positions held, who held them, and where things landed.

Centralised vs decentralised architecture
start central.shenka., bumblebee_bzzz — decentralisation needs mass adoption first; better to launch centralised and allow forking later.
fully decentraldarksecret7 — centralised = just replacing one monopoly with another. Needs a protocol, not a platform.
pragmaticelcumbiavillera2, andyrodrigues30 — no human concept is a perfect model; some centralisation is unavoidable.
Landed: "start centralised, build framework for decentralisation" (.shenka.). Earlier server polls showed decentralised preference.
How should certification work?
trust protocoldarksecret7 — crypto-style skill ledger. Peers-in-work verify you, not an org. "Blockchain for skills, not money."
volunteer jurybumblebee_bzzz — 5 randomly selected people who already passed the same test verify the next person. Minimises bias.
cost barrier.shenka. — certs need some cost as a cheating deterrent; could be minimised via volunteer live-testing but hard to scale.
work-baseddarksecret7 — the only honest way is seeing someone perform the skill. Portfolio/work evidence over tests.
Landed: unresolved. Consensus that current system is imperfect but no chosen alternative. Postponed as "later down the line."
Funding model
affiliate links.shenka. — non-intrusive subject-specific kit links (e.g. CRISPR kits under biology guides). Niche shops love this.
donationsinco0 — Wikipedia model: big email campaigns to top donors. Pairs with foundation partnerships.
sponsored contentinco0 — blood-type-test guide sponsored by the kit brand. Product placement that is the lesson itself.
scepticalshalizius7 — conversion rates will be awful for a small site; only gambling/adult ad buyers take small traffic.
Landed: still open. Affiliate links + donations + eventual grants seen as most likely combo. No formal decision.
Preventing AI slop
community votesdeathwish288 — users flag suspected AI content; threshold triggers moderator review.
human modneo_quantum — real human moderation with verification protocols. Slower but more reliable.
custom CAPTCHAplaceholder_name_123 — custom system that doesn't train AI models. Email verification as secondary layer.
face verifydeathwish288 — facial similarity to block multi-account abuse. Rejected by others as a privacy risk.
Landed: unresolved. Facial verification rejected. Human moderation + CAPTCHA most favoured but not formalised.
